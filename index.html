What is spm?

Scalp Micropigmentation (SMP) is a widely popular non-surgical hair loss solution whereby minuscule deposits of pigment simulate hair follicles on the scalp. Unlike traditional tattooing, SMP pigment contains no color-changing additives and is only deposited into the shallow epidermal layer of skin, making it easy to remove via laser. The SMP procedure revitalizes receded hairlines, adds density to thinning hair, camouflages hair transplant scarring, and treats alopecia all while remaining completely undetectable. Clients come away with a fresh and clean-shaven cropped hair look.

WHY CHOOSE SMP OVER OTHER HAIR LOSS SOLUTIONS?

Most hair loss treatments are either obvious and embarrassing (think: displaced hairpieces), expensive (hair transplants), require invasive surgery (hair transplants), or call for lifelong commitments to risky medications (Finasteride or Minoxidil). By forgoing the multiple transplants required for desirable results and the ongoing medication to prevent further hair loss, SMP gives you thousands more “follicles” without drugs or surgeries. Ironically, many hair transplant patients later use SMP to disguise scarring in the back of their heads that emerges as hair continues to thin. Not only is SMP superior to other hair loss treatments, it’s also used to salvage them. It’s simply the most effective and rewarding solution available.

“BUT A TATTOO…ON MY HEAD!?”

Let’s examine this concern. You’re likely not appalled by someone dying their hair. Adding pigment to one’s scalp is a similar means to enhancing one’s appearance. Think of it as simply an aesthetic alternative to a genetic occurrence that is otherwise out of your control.
